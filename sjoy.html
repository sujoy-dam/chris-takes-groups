Daily itinerary

Day 1: The trip begins

Arriving at Mactan-Cebu International Airport, you’ll be warmly welcomed and transferred to your hotel to settle in. In the evening, we’ll kick off the trip with a welcome dinner where you’ll get to know your fellow travelers and savor the flavors of Filipino cuisine (trust us, it’s seriously underrated!).

Day 2: Simala Shrine & sardine run

On our way to Moalboal, we’ll visit the stunning Simala Shrine, a grand and sacred site that looks straight out of a fairytale. The magic continues with the Sardine Run, a world-famous natural phenomenon where millions of sardines swirl in mesmerising, synchronised schools just off the coast. Grab your snorkel and keep an eye out; sea turtles often join the fun!.

Day 3: Canyoneering at Kawasan Falls

Many say canyoneering at Kawasan Falls is the highlight of their trip to the Philippines! This adrenaline-fueled adventure takes you through turquoise waters, lush jungle canyons, and cascading waterfalls. Brave the thrilling cliff jumps, glide down natural waterslides, and immerse yourself in nature’s playground.

Day 4: Island hop to Bohol & sunset yoga

Next, we journey to Bohol, the second island of our adventure, where we’ll stay in a serene riverside resort in Loboc. Surrounded by lush nature, it’s the perfect place to unwind after a travel day, ending with a calming sunset yoga session led by a wonderful instructor.

Day 5: Bohol countryside tuk-tuk tour

Explore the highlights of Bohol in style…by tuk-tuk! Wind through scenic palm-lined roads, stop to admire the iconic Chocolate Hills and fall in love with the world’s smallest primates at the Tarsier Sanctuary. After building an appetite, we’ll refuel with a delicious 8-course farm-to-table feast at a women-run restaurant.

Day 6: Beach clean up & travel to Siquijor

Together with the local community, we’ll help restore the beach to its natural beauty – clean and trash-free. It’s a meaningful way to connect with locals and positively impact the places we explore. After soaking in the beach vibes, we’ll catch a ferry to Siquijor, an island that’s quickly becoming one of the Philippines’ most sought-after islands.

Day 7: Island tour of Siquijor

Discover the mystical allure of Siquijor, an island steeped in legends of healing and witchcraft. Our epic island tour includes swimming in enchanting waterfalls, relaxing on pristine beaches, and wandering through charming villages before meeting a local healer. Here, we’ll experience age-old spiritual and natural healing practices, like Bolo-bolo, passed down through generations.

Day 8: Snorkelling & diving trip Apo Island

Prepare to experience some of the most incredible marine life and coral reefs you’ve ever seen. Apo Island is a world-renowned diving paradise, drawing ocean lovers from across the globe. Our boat trip will take you to the best spots, where you'll swim alongside countless sea turtles and explore a mindblowing underwater world. Grab your snorkel and fins, or take the adventure deeper with an optional scuba dive.

Day 9: Farewell dinner & karaoke

Leaving Siquijor won’t be easy, but the adventure isn’t over yet! We’ll head back to Cebu City, where we’ll spend the afternoon exploring Cebu’s best food market (Balut anyone?) before wrapping up our journey with a farewell dinner. For our final night together, we’ll celebrate Filipino style—so warm up those vocal cords because no trip is complete without karaoke!

Day 10: Time to say goodbye

It’s time to say goodbye to your awesome tour guide, fellow travellers turned new friends, and the wonderful country of the Philippines.

We’ll drop you off at Mactan-Cebu International Airport, ready for your flight home.

Frequently asked questions

What airport do I need to fly in and out of?

The trip starts and ends in Cebu City, home to Mactan-Cebu International Airport. Need a hand with your flights? We’re experts at finding cheap flight deals!

Do I need a visa?

Many countries are eligible for visa-free entry for a short period (usually 30 days). These countries include the U.S., Canada, EU countries, Australia, the UK, and others.

We always recommend checking your home country's government website for up-to-date and essential travel advice, including passport and visa information.

What meals are included?

All breakfasts, three lunches and two dinners are included in the trip price. For the rest of the meals, your local guide will take you to the best affordable spots in the area.

Can you cater for dietary requirements?

We make every effort to accommodate dietary requirements, including vegetarian and vegan options. Please inform us of any food restrictions or special dietary needs when booking so we can plan accordingly.

Will I be picked up from the airport?

We want our trips to be as stress-free as possible. Both airport pick-up and drop-off are included in the trip price.

What’s not included?

While most expenses are covered in the trip price, there are a few exclusions, including:

  • International flights to and from the Philippines
  • Visa fees (if applicable)
  • Personal travel insurance (mandatory)
  • Tips
  • Any optional activities, including scuba diving at Apo Island
  • Any meals not listed in the itinerary

Is there a payment plan?

Upon booking, a £200 (or equivalent) deposit is required to secure your spot on the trip. At this time, you can also pay for the trip in full.

Want to spread out your trip payments? We can split the cost across two payments, paid 120 days and 60 days before the trip departure date. Don’t worry, you don’t need to remember these dates, we’ll be in touch when the payments are due.

To avoid debit or credit card fees, you can pay with direct bank transfer when you get to the checkout.
